A Fashion A-Level has been announced

Fashion obsessed? Born in the millenium? The new fashion A-level might get you a step ahead of the game

Save for later

Latest News Industry Intel Lifestyle Events Intern Blogs Personal Space

If you were born in the millennium, are set to study A-Levels, and want to learn your Balenciaga from your Givenchy, then you are in luck. The AQA have announced an A-Level in Fashion will run from 2017 onwards.

It’s an opportunity granted to none before that will arm students with a strong base of knowledge, upon which they can begin to build a career in the fashion industry. For school pupils nervously awaiting years worth of internships, university courses and initally low pay, this is game-changing news that could fasttrack their success.

The A-Level will take an in-depth look at influential designers, like Chanel, Dior, Mary Quant, Vivienne Westwood and McQueen and cover major historical designs and movements like punk, Pop Art and minimalism. The course will also analyse the socio-economic influences on fashion, including street culture, social media, the role of women in society, music, World War II and the rise of youth culture.

(Choice: the new A-level will enable students to understand the different roles in fashion from an early age)

Students will take on the industry's commercial side, learning about branding and marketing. For anybody who dreams of being a designer, creating their own brand or, indeed, forging a career in fashion marketing or branding, then these are essential skills to hone. Also included on the syllabus are maths and science elements, teaching students how to work out information like how much material they need to create a design.

"It's important that we support the growth of this industry by developing young people with a solid understanding"

Ben Galvin, AQA Qualifications Developer

The design industry in the UK is worth £3.2 billion to the economy and is the fastest growing sector in the country. Ben Galvin, AQA Qualifications Developer, said, “It’s important that we support the growth of this industry by developing young people with a solid understanding of the commercial, creative and technical sides of the trade.”

This is an exciting addition from the AQA that matches up with the calibre of the British fashion industry. As a holistic course, it will provide younger aspiring fashion professionals with knowledge, understanding and choice. At 16, you shouldn't be expected to know exactly what you aspire to become; it's experience and learning that helps point you in the right direction. The Fashion A-Level seems well-set to give young fashion lovers an essential headstart in the hard-to-navigate, but rewarding industry of fashion.

Eilidh Hargreaves
